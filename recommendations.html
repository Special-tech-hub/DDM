Detailed Action Plan

Specific recommendations for each dimension, with targets and timelines.

Critical Priority

A. WiFi Reliability — Infrastructure Upgrade

Finding: WiFi is the single strongest predictor of digital maturity (~0.90 feature importance). Established students rate it at only 2.24/5 — the largest gap in the dataset.

Actions:

  • Increase campus bandwidth and deploy Wi-Fi 6 access points in high-density areas
  • Extend coverage to hostels, lecture rooms, and library study areas
  • Implement QoS policies to prioritise academic traffic over general browsing
  • Introduce a real-time network status dashboard for students
Target: Raise average WiFi score from 2.24 to ≥4.0 within 12 months
High Priority

B. Emhare Portal — UX Redesign

Finding: All groups score Emhare at 3.0–3.4. It is functional but not satisfying. Emerging students find it slightly easier (3.41) while Established students are more critical (3.21).

Actions:

  • Conduct a full UX audit with student focus groups
  • Simplify registration and results-checking workflows to fewer steps
  • Add mobile-responsive design and offline caching for low-connectivity scenarios
  • Improve error messages and add contextual help tooltips
Target: Raise Emhare score to ≥4.0 across all groups by Q3
High Priority

C. Clearance Process — Full Automation

Finding: ClearanceProcess scores ~3.0–3.1 across all groups — a shared, consistent pain point. ICT Staff score it lowest at 2.0, indicating backend inefficiencies.

Actions:

  • Automate clearance end-to-end — integrate library, finance, and academic registry systems
  • Introduce real-time clearance status tracking visible to students
  • Eliminate manual sign-off steps; replace with digital approvals
  • Set SLA targets for each clearance stage (e.g., 24-hour turnaround)
Target: Reduce manual steps by 80%; raise score to ≥4.0 by Q4
Medium Priority

D. Google Classroom — Digital Literacy Training

Finding: Confidence grows with maturity (Established: 3.27 vs Emerging: 3.07). This is a quick win — low effort, meaningful impact on Emerging students.

Actions:

  • Run structured digital literacy workshops for Emerging students each semester
  • Create short video tutorials and embed them in the Emhare portal
  • Establish a peer-mentoring programme pairing Established with Emerging students
  • Integrate Google Classroom orientation into student induction week
Target: Raise Emerging students' confidence to ≥3.8 by end of Q2
Medium Priority

E. Librarians & ICT Staff — Capacity Building

Finding: All Librarians and ICT Staff are classified as Established — a positive baseline — but scores are uniformly 3.0, indicating a ceiling effect with room for growth.

Actions:

  • Invest in advanced ICT training: cybersecurity, cloud systems, data analytics
  • Librarians: training on digital resource management, e-learning support, and metadata standards
  • ICT Staff: training on network management, helpdesk automation, and system integration
  • Establish annual upskilling certification programme
Target: Move all staff to Advanced tier through annual upskilling by end of year
